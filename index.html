Some things about me...

I'm 19 years old and I'm currently in my second year of Biology at university. I'm interested in animal behaviour and more specifically the relationship between animals and humans. I'm very passionate about animal welfare and animal rights. I'm a vegetarian, working on slowly making the transition to the vegan lifestyle.
The past few months I've been standing up against animal dissection and use of animal samples in the lab lessons of my course. This is something I didn't know was happening at my own univesity until this past September. I am being met with claims that my university is unable to change the teaching methods that are being used at the moment and that I am unable to finish this course without participating in lab work that involves some kind of animal samples. I am therefore looking for alternatives and ways of making a difference in the area of animal welfare.
